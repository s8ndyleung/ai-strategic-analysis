# Deliverable Generator Rule

When the user says "generate deliverables" or after all research stages complete, produce final stakeholder-ready output from the research artifacts.

---

## Your Job

Take research output files and produce consolidated deliverable pages that synthesize the analysis into executive-ready narratives.

---

## Deliverable Pages

Map research stages to consolidated deliverable sections:

| Research Stages | Deliverable Section |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Market & Competitive Intelligence | Market & Competitive Landscape |
| Situation Assessment | Strategy & Assessment |
| Market Sizing & Financial Model + Business Case | Business Case & Financials |
| Recommendation & Roadmap | Recommendation |

Each section synthesizes the underlying research into a single executive narrative. Do not dump raw research — structure content for a consulting deliverable.

---

## Structure

### Overview / Executive Summary

The first thing a reader sees. It must answer: *what did we find, and what should we do?*

1. **Title and framing:** The core question (not the answer).
2. **Governing thought:** The recommendation in one sentence.
3. **Key metrics (4–5 max):** TAM, expected revenue (labeled P&L), investment required, payback period, expected value.
4. **Navigation:** Links to each section.

### Section Pages

Each section follows this pattern:

1. **Governing thought** — One sentence that captures the section's conclusion.
2. **Key findings** — 3–5 bullets, each with evidence.
3. **Supporting detail** — Tables, charts (described), comparisons.
4. **Implications** — What this means for the recommendation.

---

## Output Format Options

Choose based on the engagement brief's deliverable format:

| Format | When to Use | Tools |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **HTML** | Web-publishable, shareable via URL | Write responsive HTML with inline styles |
| **Markdown** | Internal review, iterative drafts | Plain markdown files |
| **Deck-ready** | Presentation to leadership | Storyboard in markdown → convert with slide tool |

---

## Quality Standards

1. **Content parity.** Every key finding from research appears in deliverables. Nothing invented, nothing dropped.
2. **Numerical consistency.** Every number in deliverables traces to a research file. Cross-check before publishing.
3. **Executive-ready language.** No jargon, no throat-clearing, no filler. Answer first, support after.
4. **Visual hierarchy.** Use headings, tables, and whitespace. A scanning reader gets the gist in 30 seconds.
5. **Source attribution.** Key claims reference their source. The reader knows this isn't opinion.
